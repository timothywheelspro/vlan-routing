**Documents Needed**: Bank statements (last 12 months), credit card statements

## üéØ What Fees to Look For

### NSF Fees (Non-Sufficient Funds)
- **What it is**: Charged when you don't have enough money in your account to cover a transaction
- **Typical cost**: $25-$35 per occurrence
- **Also called**: Insufficient funds fee, bounced check fee, returned item fee

### Overdraft Fees
- **What it is**: Similar to NSF, but bank covers the transaction and charges you
- **Typical cost**: $30-$40 per occurrence
- **Also called**: Overdraft protection fee, overdraft transfer fee

### Monthly Maintenance Fees
- **What it is**: Monthly fee for having the account
- **Typical cost**: $5-$25/month
- **Also called**: Monthly service fee, account maintenance fee

### ATM Fees
- **What it is**: Fee for using out-of-network ATMs
- **Typical cost**: $2-$5 per transaction
- **Also called**: ATM surcharge, foreign ATM fee

### Other Common Fees
- **Wire transfer fees**: $15-$50 per transfer
- **Stop payment fees**: $25-$35 per request
- **Returned deposit fees**: $10-$15 per item
- **Account closure fees**: $5-$25 (if closing too soon)
- **Minimum balance fees**: Charged if account falls below minimum
- **Paper statement fees**: $2-$5/month (for paper statements)
- **Account research fees**: $25-$50/hour
- **Cashier's check fees**: $5-$15 per check
- **Money order fees**: $1-$5 per order

## üîç How to Find Fees on Different Statement Formats

### Online Banking Statements

**Method 1: Transaction List**
1. Log into online banking
2. Go to "Account History" or "Transactions"
3. Filter by date range (last 12 months)
4. Look for negative amounts with fee-related descriptions
5. Download or export statement if available

**Method 2: Search Function**
1. Use search/filter function
2. Search for keywords: "fee", "NSF", "overdraft", "service"
3. Review all results

**Method 3: Fee Summary**
- Some banks have a "Fee Summary" or "Service Charges" section
- Check account details/settings page

### PDF Statements

1. Open PDF statement
2. Use Ctrl+F (Cmd+F on Mac) to search for:
   - "fee"
   - "NSF"
   - "overdraft"
   - "service"
   - "$" (then review negative amounts)
3. Review each page systematically
4. Note page number where fee appears (for reference)

### Paper Statements

1. Go through each statement page by page
2. Look in transaction sections
3. Check for separate "Fees" or "Service Charges" section
4. Highlight or mark each fee as you find it
5. Record on tracking sheet

## üéØ Fee Analysis Questions

For each fee you find, ask:

1. **Was it avoidable?**
   - Could you have prevented it?
   - Was it due to poor planning or oversight?

2. **What caused it?**
   - Timing issue (bill due before paycheck)?
   - Forgot about a payment?
   - Didn't know balance was low?

3. **Could you have prevented it?**
   - Set up low balance alert?
   - Better cash flow management?
   - Different account setup?

4. **Is it recurring?**
   - Does it happen every month?
   - Does it happen at same time each month?
   - Is there a pattern?

## üìä Pattern Recognition

After recording all fees, look for patterns:

### Common Patterns:

**Pattern 1: Timing Issues**
- Fees always happen on certain dates
- Bills due before payday
- **Solution**: Adjust bill due dates or build buffer

**Pattern 2: Forgetting Payments**
- Fees from auto-pay you forgot about
- **Solution**: Set reminders, use calendar, track better

**Pattern 3: Low Balance**
- Account frequently runs low
- **Solution**: Build account buffer, use savings account

**Pattern 4: Monthly Fees**
- Paying monthly maintenance fees
- **Solution**: Switch to fee-free account or meet minimum balance requirements

**Pattern 5: ATM Fees**
- Frequently using out-of-network ATMs
- **Solution**: Use in-network ATMs, get cash back at stores, switch banks

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Do credit cards have NSF fees?**  
A: No, but they have late fees, over-limit fees, etc. Check credit card statements separately.

**Q: What if I can't find old statements?**  
A: Use what you have (even 3-6 months is useful). Contact bank for older statements if needed.

**Q: Should I include fees that were refunded?**  
A: Note them but don't count in total (since they were refunded). They still show a problem pattern.

**Q: What about business account fees?**  
A: Yes, include them. Track business and personal separately if helpful.

**Q: How do I prevent these fees in the future?**  
A: Build account buffer ($500-$1,000+), set up alerts, use fee-free accounts, better cash flow management.

**Q: My bank charges monthly fees but I can't avoid them. What do I do?**  
A: Include them in your budget as a fixed expense. But research if you can switch to a fee-free account or meet minimum balance requirements.
