# How to Fill Out the Income Section
**Step-by-Step Guide with Examples**

---

## üìã Overview

The income section is the foundation of your budget. You need to accurately track all money coming in so you can compare it to expenses and calculate your cash flow.

**Time Required**: 15-30 minutes  
**Documents Needed**: Pay stubs, bank statements, tax documents (if available)

---

## üéØ Step-by-Step Instructions

### Step 1: Gather Your Documents (5 minutes)

Collect the following:
- [ ] **Pay stubs** - Last 3 months (shows regular paychecks)
- [ ] **Bank statements** - Last 3 months (shows deposits)
- [ ] **1099 forms** (if freelance/contractor) - Last year
- [ ] **W-2 forms** (if employee) - Last year
- [ ] **Side income records** - Freelance, gig work, etc.
- [ ] **Other income** - Investments, rental income, etc.

### Step 2: Identify All Income Sources (10 minutes)

List every source of money that comes in regularly:

#### Primary Income Sources:
- [ ] **Employment salary/wages** - Your main job
- [ ] **Hourly wages** - If paid hourly
- [ ] **Freelance/contract work** - 1099 income
- [ ] **Business income** - If you own a business

#### Secondary Income Sources:
- [ ] **Side jobs/gig work** - Uber, DoorDash, etc.
- [ ] **Part-time job** - Second job income
- [ ] **Investment income** - Dividends, interest
- [ ] **Rental income** - Property rental
- [ ] **Pension/retirement** - If applicable
- [ ] **Social Security** - If applicable
- [ ] **VA benefits** - Disability, education, etc.
- [ ] **Unemployment** - If currently receiving
- [ ] **Child support/alimony** - If receiving
- [ ] **Other regular income** - List anything else

### Step 3: Calculate Monthly Income (10-15 minutes)

#### For Regular Paychecks (Bi-weekly, Semi-monthly, Weekly):

**Bi-weekly (every 2 weeks = 26 paychecks/year):**
```
Monthly Income = (Paycheck Amount √ó 26) √∑ 12

Example: $2,000 bi-weekly paycheck
Monthly = ($2,000 √ó 26) √∑ 12 = $52,000 √∑ 12 = $4,333.33/month
```

**Semi-monthly (twice per month = 24 paychecks/year):**
```
Monthly Income = Paycheck Amount √ó 2

Example: $2,500 semi-monthly paycheck
Monthly = $2,500 √ó 2 = $5,000/month
```

**Weekly (every week = 52 paychecks/year):**
```
Monthly Income = (Weekly Pay √ó 52) √∑ 12

Example: $1,000 weekly paycheck
Monthly = ($1,000 √ó 52) √∑ 12 = $52,000 √∑ 12 = $4,333.33/month
```

**Monthly (once per month):**
```
Monthly Income = Paycheck Amount

Example: $5,000 monthly paycheck
Monthly = $5,000/month
```

#### For Irregular Income (Freelance, Gig Work):

**Option 1: Average Last 3-6 Months**
```
Monthly Average = Sum of last 3-6 months √∑ Number of months

Example:
Month 1: $3,000
Month 2: $2,500
Month 3: $3,500
Average = ($3,000 + $2,500 + $3,500) √∑ 3 = $3,000/month
```

**Option 2: Conservative Estimate**
- Use the LOWEST month from past 6 months
- This prevents overestimating income

## üìä Income Calculation Examples

### Example 1: Salaried Employee

**Situation:**
- Salary: $75,000/year
- Paid: Semi-monthly (24 paychecks)
- Take-home after taxes/insurance: $2,400 per paycheck

**Calculation:**
```
Monthly Income = $2,400 √ó 2 = $4,800/month
Annual Income = $4,800 √ó 12 = $57,600/year (after taxes)
```

**Template Entry:**
```
| Source | Amount | Frequency | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Software Engineer | $4,800 | Semi-monthly | After taxes, health, 401k |
| **TOTAL MONTHLY INCOME** | **$4,800** | | |
```

### Example 2: Hourly Worker + Side Gig

**Situation:**
- Full-time job: $25/hour, 40 hours/week
- Paid: Weekly
- Take-home: $750/week after taxes
- Side gig (Uber): Averages $300/week
- Take-home from Uber: $250/week (after gas/expenses)

**Calculation:**
```
Full-time monthly = ($750 √ó 52) √∑ 12 = $3,250/month
Side gig monthly = ($250 √ó 52) √∑ 12 = $1,083/month
Total = $3,250 + $1,083 = $4,333/month
```

**Template Entry:**
```
| Source | Amount | Frequency | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Warehouse Job | $3,250 | Weekly | After taxes, 40 hrs/week |
| Uber Driving | $1,083 | Weekly | Average after gas/expenses |
| **TOTAL MONTHLY INCOME** | **$4,333** | | |
```

### Example 3: Freelancer with Variable Income

**Situation:**
- Freelance designer
- Last 6 months: $2,000, $3,500, $2,800, $4,200, $3,000, $3,200
- Very irregular, but generally increasing

**Calculation:**
```
Average = ($2,000 + $3,500 + $2,800 + $4,200 + $3,000 + $3,200) √∑ 6
Average = $18,700 √∑ 6 = $3,117/month
```

**Use conservative estimate:** Use $3,000/month (rounded down)

**Template Entry:**
```
| Source | Amount | Frequency | Notes |
|--------|--------|-----------|-------|
| Freelance Design | $3,000 | Monthly | Average of last 6 months, conservative |
| **TOTAL MONTHLY INCOME** | **$3,000** | | |
```

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Common Mistakes to Avoid

### ‚ùå Mistake 1: Using Gross (Before-Tax) Income
**Wrong:** "I make $75,000/year, so $6,250/month"  
**Right:** Use take-home pay after taxes, insurance, 401k deductions

**How to find take-home:**
- Look at your bank deposit (what actually hits your account)
- Check pay stub "Net Pay" or "Take Home" line
- Don't use "Gross Pay"

### ‚ùå Mistake 2: Overestimating Variable Income
**Wrong:** "I made $5,000 last month from freelancing, so I'll use that"  
**Right:** Average several months, or use a conservative (low) estimate

**Why it matters:** If you budget based on your best month, you'll be short when you have a bad month.

### ‚ùå Mistake 3: Counting Uncertain Income
**Wrong:** "I might get a $5,000 bonus, so I'll include it"  
**Right:** Only count income you're CERTAIN will happen

**Rule:** If it's not guaranteed, don't count it. If it happens, it's a bonus you can save or use for debt.

### ‚ùå Mistake 4: Forgetting About Deductions
**Wrong:** "My salary is $60,000, so $5,000/month"  
**Right:** Account for taxes, health insurance, 401k, etc.

**Tip:** Your bank statement shows the real number - use that!

### ‚ùå Mistake 5: Including Business Revenue Instead of Profit
**Wrong:** "My business makes $10,000/month in revenue"  
**Right:** Use profit after business expenses

**For business owners:** Income = Revenue - Business Expenses

## ‚úÖ Verification Checklist

After filling out the income section, verify:

- [ ] All income sources listed
- [ ] Using AFTER-TAX amounts (take-home pay)
- [ ] Monthly amounts calculated correctly

- [ ] Total matches what you actually see in your bank account

- [ ] Variable income averaged or estimated conservatively

### Quick Verification Test:

**Does your monthly income total match what you see in your bank account?**

Add up all deposits to your checking account over the past 3 months, then divide by 3. This should be close to your calculated monthly income (within 5-10%).

```
Example:
Month 1 deposits: $7,200
Month 2 deposits: $7,100
Month 3 deposits: $7,300
Average: ($7,200 + $7,100 + $7,300) √∑ 3 = $7,200/month

Your calculated income should be around $7,200/month
```

## ‚ùì Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: Should I include my tax refund as monthly income?**  
A: No, it's one-time. Include it in the "Annual/One-Time Income" section, not monthly.

**Q: My hours vary - how do I calculate monthly income?**  
A: Average your last 3-6 months of take-home pay. Be conservative (use the lower months).

**Q: I'm self-employed - gross revenue or profit?**  
A: Use PROFIT (revenue minus business expenses). Your personal income is what you pay yourself.

**Q: Should I include investment gains?**  
A: Only if they're regular and reliable (like dividend payments). Don't include stock market gains unless you're regularly selling.

**Q: My spouse and I have separate accounts - do I include their income?**  
A: Only if you're doing a combined household budget. If separate finances, only include your own income.

**Q: I get paid bi-weekly - why is the calculation different?**  
A: Bi-weekly = every 2 weeks = 26 paychecks/year (not 24). Two months per year you get 3 paychecks instead of 2, which is why you divide by 12 instead of multiplying by 2.
